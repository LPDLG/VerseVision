# Project Profile
This repository is used for presentations on the VerseVision project. Here, we provide representative samples from our Chinese poetry-to-landscape painting generation dataset, including original Chinese landscape paintings, corresponding classical poem prompts, detailed textual descriptions, user-specified layout bounding boxes, depth maps, and line/edge maps for structural supervision.
# VerseVision
Generating Chinese landscape paintings from classical poetry is a challenging task in text-to-image generation. Unlike ordinary text prompts, Chinese poems are highly condensed, implicit, and rich in symbolic imagery. A single verse may imply mountains, rivers, pavilions, boats, trees, mist, or human figures, while the spatial layout and artistic conception are often not explicitly described. Existing generative models often suffer from weak layout controllability, inaccurate object placement, blurred local structures, and insufficient preservation of traditional Chinese landscape painting aesthetics.
To address these challenges, we propose VerseVision, a layout-guided Chinese poetry-to-landscape painting generation framework. The method takes classical Chinese poetry, detailed semantic descriptions, and user-specified bounding boxes as input, and generates controllable Chinese landscape paintings with improved semantic consistency, spatial layout accuracy, and artistic structure.
First, we introduce a Feathered Soft-Hard Layout Grounding mechanism. This module uses user-provided bounding boxes to construct hard spatial constraints and soft feathered transition regions inside the GLIGEN fuser attention module. The hard mask improves local semantic grounding, while the feathered residual modulation enables smoother boundary fusion between foreground objects and the surrounding landscape.
Second, we propose Poetic Landscape Structural Prior Control, termed PLSP-Control. The user layout is converted into multi-channel structural prior maps, including semantic layout channels and landscape structure channels. These priors are injected into the multi-scale UNet backbone, allowing the model to better control global composition, object distribution, ink density, blank-leaving regions, and landscape hierarchy.
Furthermore, we introduce Teacher-Supervised Structural Distillation. During training, external depth maps and line/edge maps are used as teacher signals to supervise the PLSP branch. This encourages the model to learn more accurate spatial depth, contour structure, and brushwork-like boundary information. The teacher maps are used only during training and are not required during inference.
More importantly, we construct a Chinese poetry-landscape painting dataset, termed VerseLand. The dataset contains Chinese landscape paintings paired with classical poem prompts, detailed image descriptions, object-level layout annotations, and auxiliary structural maps. These annotations provide reliable support for layout-controllable and structure-aware Chinese landscape painting generation.
Experimental results show that VerseVision improves layout controllability, poem-image semantic consistency, and traditional landscape painting structure compared with baseline diffusion models. Under user-specified layout conditions, the proposed method produces more coherent mountains, rivers, trees, pavilions, boats, and other landscape elements, while preserving the poetic atmosphere of classical Chinese verse.
